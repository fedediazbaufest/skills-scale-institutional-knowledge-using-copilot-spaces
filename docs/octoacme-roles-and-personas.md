# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
Designs user experiences, produces wireframes and UI artifacts, and helps define usability requirements. Ensures that delivered features are intuitive and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Collaborate with Product Manager to clarify requirements and user journeys
- Produce wireframes, mockups, and design specifications for Developers
- Work with Developers on feasibility and iterative design changes
- Participate in sprint reviews and demos to provide and gather design feedback
- Conduct or coordinate usability testing and incorporate findings

### Goals
- Deliver consistent, accessible, and user-centric designs
- Reduce rework by clarifying UI expectations early in each sprint
- Maintain a shared design system or style guide

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques with Developers and Product Manager
- Annotations and specs in design tooling (e.g., Figma)
- Participation in sprint planning and review sessions

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Sets technical direction and architectural standards, reviews proposed solutions, and guides the engineering team through complex decisions. Acts as the bridge between project delivery goals and technical feasibility.

### Responsibilities
- Approve technical designs and assist in identifying technical risks
- Support Developers in estimation, task breakdown, and architecture decisions
- Work closely with Project Manager on timelines, dependencies, and escalations
- Consult with Product Manager on feasibility trade-offs
- Lead technical design reviews and code quality standards

### Goals
- Maintain a coherent, scalable, and maintainable architecture
- Reduce technical debt introduced during delivery
- Enable the team to deliver with confidence and speed

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)
- Participation in planning, risk reviews, and escalations
- Code review feedback and pairing sessions with Developers

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Defines the test strategy, oversees QA practices, and ensures quality gates are met before releases. Works to embed quality throughout the delivery lifecycle rather than only at the end.

### Responsibilities
- Craft test plans in collaboration with Product Manager and Developers
- Coordinate bug triage and regression testing across sprints
- Review and update acceptance criteria for clarity and testability
- Define and maintain the Definition of Done quality standards
- Coordinate pre-release smoke and acceptance tests

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Improve test coverage and reduce manual testing burden over time
- Ensure all acceptance criteria are verifiable and verified

### Typical Communication
- Regular syncs with Developers and Project Manager
- Test plans and bug reports in the project tracking system
- Participation in sprint reviews and release readiness checks

---

## Support/Customer Success

### Role Summary
Bridges feedback from customers and users back into the continuous improvement cycle. Advocates for the user experience post-release and ensures the team is aware of real-world usage impacts.

### Responsibilities
- Collect, summarize, and communicate user issues and improvement requests to the Product Manager
- Support incident communication and post-release user care messaging
- Coordinate with Product Lead on known issues, workarounds, and planned fixes
- Participate in incident review cycles to ensure customer impact is captured

### Goals
- Close the feedback loop between users and the development team
- Reduce time-to-resolution for customer-impacting issues
- Ensure release communications and support materials are ready before launch

### Typical Communication
- Regular syncs with Product Manager on user feedback and support trends
- Participation in incident retrospectives and release planning
- Drafting user-facing release notes and support documentation

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
Ensures security considerations are included at all stages of delivery. Acts as a security advisor embedded within the team, helping to identify and mitigate risks before they become vulnerabilities.

### Responsibilities
- Advise on secure coding practices and review implementation for potential vulnerabilities
- Review releases for security risks and coordinate with the Security team when needed
- Participate in risk reviews and threat modeling sessions during planning
- Coordinate incident response when security issues are identified in production
- Ensure security scanning is integrated in CI and findings are actioned

### Goals
- Shift security left — catch issues in design and development, not in production
- Maintain compliance with security standards and policies
- Build security awareness across the team

### Typical Communication
- Security review feedback during PR reviews and design sessions
- Participation in risk register updates and release readiness checks
- Coordination with Developers, Project Manager, and QA on risk mitigation
