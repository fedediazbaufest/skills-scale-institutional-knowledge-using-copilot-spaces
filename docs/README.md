## OctoAcme Project Management Philosophy

OctoAcme delivers projects using a **customer-centric** approach focused on clarity, incremental delivery, and continuous improvement. Our process is built on five core principles:

- **Customer-first:** prioritize customer value and usability.
- **Iterative delivery:** deliver small, testable increments.
- **Clear ownership:** each project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead.
- **Data-informed decisions:** measure impact and iterate based on evidence.
- **Psychological safety:** encourage feedback and learning.

## Project Management Stages

### 1. Initiation

Align on the problem, business needs, and stakeholders — and decide go/no-go before committing to planning. Key outputs include a Project One-pager, stakeholder list, high-level timeline, initial risk list, and a resource estimate. A decision gate confirms that success metrics are clear, stakeholders agree on priority, and team availability is confirmed before moving forward.

### 2. Planning

Turn an approved initiative into an actionable backlog and delivery plan. Activities include a kickoff meeting, backlog creation with acceptance criteria, scope estimation, Definition of Done documentation, dependency identification, and a release plan with milestone map.

### 3. Execution & Tracking

Manage day-to-day delivery through structured team rhythms (daily standups, weekly syncs, sprint demos), a project board workflow, pull request conventions, quality and testing practices, and a clear blocker escalation path. Key metrics include velocity, burndown, and success metrics from the Project One-pager.

### 4. Risk Management & Communication

Proactively identify, assess, mitigate, and monitor risks using a Risk Register. Maintain clear and regular stakeholder communication using weekly status updates, incident communication templates, and defined escalation paths (Team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor).

### 5. Release & Deployment

Standardize releases to production to reduce risk and improve observability. Covers release types (patch, minor, major), pre-release requirements (all acceptance criteria met, passing CI, release notes drafted, rollback plan documented), a deployment checklist, rollback and incident playbook, and release notes template.

### 6. Retrospective & Continuous Improvement

Capture learnings after each sprint, release, or milestone and convert them into actionable improvements. Structure includes what went well, what could be improved, and action items with owners and due dates. Action items feed back into the project backlog and are reviewed in weekly PM syncs.

### 7. Roles & Personas

Definitions of the main roles and their responsibilities within OctoAcme projects:

- **Developers** — Design, build, test, and deliver software components.
- **Product Managers** — Define what should be built, own the product vision, and prioritize the backlog.
- **Project Managers** — Coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications.
